REFERRAL SUMMARY (from Dr. Singh, Oct 12, 2025):
New-onset paw licking and facial pruritus. Single Cytopoint injection administered 10/12/2025.
Partial response noted per owner phone follow-up (10/28): pruritus reduced ~60%, returning near
end of 4-week window. Prior otitis historia: recurrent bilateral otitis externa (Jun & Aug 2025),
associated Malassezia/bacteria. Requesting allergy workup and long-term management plan.

HISTORY (owner-reported):
- Pruritus onset approximately September 2025; worsening through October.
- Primarily affects: paws (licking), periocular area, ventral abdomen.
- Seasonal pattern suspected: worse in fall, improved in winter previously.
- No dietary changes in past 12 months. Fed Royal Canin Large Breed Adult.
- No response to OTC antihistamines tried at home.
- Cytopoint (Oct 12) gave partial, temporary relief (~4–5 weeks).

PHYSICAL EXAMINATION:
Temperature: 101.5°F | Weight: 63.5 lbs
- Skin: mild hyperpigmentation and lichenification bilateral paws; alopecia periorbital bilateral;
  no pyoderma or Malassezia at this visit.
- Ears: clear bilaterally (per otoscope). Owner confirms no ear recurrence since ER visit.
- Coat: generally good condition.

INTRADERMAL ALLERGY TESTING:
Testing performed with 58-allergen panel (grasses, trees, molds, dust mites, insects, storage mites).

POSITIVE REACTIONS (2+ or greater on 0–4 scale):
- Bermuda grass (3+)
- Timothy grass (3+)
- Dermatophagoides farinae [dust mite] (2+)
- Dermatophagoides pteronyssinus [dust mite] (2+)
- Alternaria alternata [mold] (2+)
- Cladosporium (2+)

DIAGNOSIS:
Canine atopic dermatitis — environmental allergen sensitization (grass + dust mite + mold panel).
Consistent with recurrent otitis pattern (atopy is leading predisposing factor for otitis in goldens).

TREATMENT PLAN:

1. Allergen-Specific Immunotherapy (ASIT):
   Custom allergen serum formulated for Bermuda grass, Timothy grass, dust mites (D. farinae +
   D. pteronyssinus), Alternaria, Cladosporium.
   Protocol: Induction injections weekly x 12 weeks, then biweekly maintenance.
   Owner trained on subcutaneous injection technique.
   Rx #SAD-2025-1203-ASIT

2. Cytopoint (lokivetmab) 40mg SC — maintenance dosing:
   Rx #SAD-2025-1203-C
   Administer every 4–8 weeks as needed for breakthrough pruritus during induction phase.
   May be administered at Westside Vet or here.

3. Environmental management:
   - Wash bedding weekly in hot water (dust mites)
   - HEPA air purifier in sleeping area
   - Wipe paws after outdoor walks (grass exposure)
   - Avoid walking on freshly cut grass during high pollen season (spring/fall)

4. Ear prevention: Continue regular ear cleaning (enzymatic) monthly given atopy-otitis link.
   Prompt evaluation if ear symptoms return — do not delay.

FOLLOW-UP:
Recheck in 8 weeks to assess immunotherapy response and pruritus control.
Coordinate with Dr. Singh at Westside for primary care management.
